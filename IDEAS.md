# FushaLab — Feature Ideas

Collected ideas for the future of the app. Roughly grouped by theme.

---

## 1. Vocabulary Learning ⭐ (priority idea)

**The problem:** Learners hit a text and encounter 10+ unknown words. They can't read fluently, get discouraged, and don't absorb the text properly. Introducing key vocabulary *before* the text — or giving access to word meanings *during* reading — would change the experience completely.

### 1a. Pre-reading Vocabulary Panel

Before the reader shows the full text, display a small set of key words from that text (5–8 words). For each word:

```
كَتَبَ       ← Arabic with full harakat
napisao je   ← Bosnian
he wrote     ← English (toggleable)
[audio icon] ← TTS pronunciation
```

User can skip it and jump straight to the text, or spend 30 seconds on it first.

**Data:** Words would be stored in the item's JSON as a new optional field `"vocabulary": [...]`. Can be generated by the same Gemini pipeline.

**Implementation notes:**
- New `VocabularyPanel` component, shown as a step before the TextCard, or as a collapsible section above it
- Opt-in via a setting (some learners don't want it)
- The vocabulary list could also appear as a tab after reading ("Review words from this text")

### 1b. Tap-to-Look-Up (Word Tooltips)

In the reader, tap/click any Arabic word to see its root, meaning, and grammatical form in a small popup. Like LingQ.

**Implementation:**
- Tokenize the Arabic text by splitting on spaces
- Wrap each token in a `<span>` that is clickable
- On click: show a MUI `Popover` or `Tooltip` with meaning
- Word meanings: either stored per-text (from generation) or fetched from a small curated word dictionary

**Challenges:** Arabic word tokenization + root detection is non-trivial. Start with exact-match from the pre-reading vocabulary list.

### 1c. Personal Word List (Vocabulary Book)

Let users save words they want to remember while reading. A "save" icon appears on each word tooltip. Saved words go to a vocabulary list stored in localStorage.

- Accessible from the main nav
- Shows Arabic word + translation + the text it came from (with link back)
- Export to Anki format (plain CSV) as a stretch goal

### 1d. Flashcard Review Mode

Review saved (or all) vocabulary for a text or level in a simple flashcard session.

- Show Arabic → user thinks → reveal Bosnian/English
- Mark "I knew it" or "I didn't" (basic SRS: known words come up less)
- No complex algorithm needed at first — even random order with a known/unknown split is useful
- Route: `/vocab` for the personal word list, `/vocab/review` for the flashcard session

## 2. Reader Improvements

### 2a. Sentence-by-Sentence Mode

Instead of showing the full text at once, reveal it sentence by sentence. User reads one sentence, optionally hears audio for it, then presses "Next sentence". Better for C1/C2 texts which are long and complex.

### 2b. Font Size Control

A simple slider or +/- buttons for the Arabic font size. Useful on mobile where the default size may be too small or too large. Save preference to localStorage.

### 2c. Reading Timer (optional, off by default)

Track how long the user spends on each text. Could feed into a stats page later. No judgment — just a data point.

### 2d. Personal Notes on a Text

A small textarea below each text where the user can write their own observations, grammar notes, vocabulary they found interesting. Saved to localStorage keyed by item ID.

### 2e. Listening Mode

A mode where audio plays first and the Arabic text is hidden. User tries to understand from audio only, then reveals the text. Good for advanced learners. Toggle via a button in the reader header.

## 3. Progress & Gamification

### 3a. Stats Page

A dashboard showing:
- Total texts read (easy-rated)
- Texts by category and level
- Simple chart: progress toward 100 per level
- Streak: days in a row with at least one text rated

Route: `/stats`

### 3b. Streak Counter

Track daily reading activity (days where at least one text was rated). Show a flame icon + number in the header or on the home page. Purely localStorage-based, no backend needed.

### 3c. Level Completion Badge

When a user completes all texts in a level (all rated Easy), show a small celebration animation and a badge on the level chip. Encourages working through a full level.

## 4. Content & Categories

### 4a. Proverbs Category (أمثال)

Short, punchy Arabic proverbs with explanation and cultural context. Perfect for B1/B2. Each text would be the proverb + 3–5 sentences explaining its meaning and usage. Very shareable content.

### 4b. Business Arabic Category

Formal business register: meetings, emails, negotiations, contracts. Good for B2/C1 learners who want professional Arabic.

### 4c. Science & Nature Category

Scientific descriptions of natural phenomena, animals, geography — in accessible MSA. Good for expanding technical vocabulary at B2/C1.

### 4d. Short Stories (قصص قصيرة)

Narrative fiction texts — 2–4 paragraphs, a character, a simple plot, a resolution. Better than fragmented passages; learners get emotional investment in the story.

### 4e. Historical Texts

Simplified versions of classical Arabic historical accounts (Ibn Battuta, Ibn Khaldun). Introduces classical vocabulary in context while staying readable.

## 5. Search & Navigation

### 5a. Tag-Based Filtering

In the level list, show a row of tag chips that can be toggled. Clicking "airport" shows only texts tagged with airport. Good when a level has 100 texts — you can find the topic you want.

### 5b. Search Across All Texts

A search box (on the home page or as a dedicated page) that searches across all index.json files by Arabic word or English/Bosnian keyword. Useful for finding a text about a specific topic.

### 5c. "Random Text" Button

One-click to get a random text from any category/level matching your current filter. Good for daily practice without choosing.

### 5d. Continue Where You Left Off

On the home page, show a "Continue" section: the last text you were reading, with a direct link back. Read from localStorage.

## 6. UX & Accessibility

### 6a. Keyboard Shortcuts

- `Space` → reveal/hide translation
- `ArrowLeft` / `ArrowRight` → previous / next text
- `R` → replay audio
- `H` → toggle harakat
- Small keyboard shortcut hint on first visit

### 6b. Offline Support (PWA)

Add a service worker so the app works offline. Since all content is static JSON, this is achievable. Learners on mobile could download a level and study on the metro without internet.

### 6c. Onboarding / Level Placement

A short 5-question quiz on first visit to suggest which level to start at. Show one text from each level, ask "How comfortable is this?" Redirect to the recommended level. Store in localStorage, skippable.

### 6d. Grammar Tips (Optional Tooltips)

For certain tagged grammar structures (e.g. texts tagged `passive-voice`, `conditional`), show a small collapsible panel explaining that grammar structure. A learning nudge without turning the app into a grammar textbook.

---

## 7. Social / Sharing

### 7a. Share a Text

A "Share" button on each text that generates a link directly to that text (already possible via URL, just needs a visible button + copy-to-clipboard). The URL like `/learn/travel/B1/travel-b1-042` already works.

### 7b. Text-as-Image Export

Generate a card image of the Arabic text (Arabic + translation) for sharing on Instagram/WhatsApp. Could use `html2canvas` or a Canvas-based approach.

## 8. Content Pipeline

### 8a. Vocabulary Extraction in Generation

When Gemini generates a text, also ask it to extract 5–8 key vocabulary items from that text and include them in the JSON. Extend the item format:

```json
"vocabulary": [
  { "arabic": "سَافَرَ", "translation": "putovati", "translationEn": "to travel", "root": "س ف ر" },
  ...
]
```

This feeds directly into feature 1a and 1b without any extra lookup logic.

### 8b. Grammar Tag Extraction

Ask Gemini to also tag the dominant grammar structures in each text (e.g. `passive-voice`, `relative-clause`, `conditional`). This enriches the filtering (feature 5a) and grammar tips (feature 6d).

### 8c. Difficulty Score Refinement

The current `difficulty` is just a level-based number (1, 2, 3). A more granular score (1–10) based on sentence length, vocabulary frequency, and structure complexity could let us sort texts within a level from easier to harder — good for a progressive path through 100 texts.
